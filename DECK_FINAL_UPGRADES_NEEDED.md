**Risk 3: Incumbent Competition (Careem/Noon Launch Copycat)**

**Likelihood:** High (70% within 12-18 months if we succeed)

**Mitigation:**
- **Speed to market:** Launch in 7 days, hit 10K users in 90 days (first-mover advantage)
- **Merchant lock-in:** Exclusive 12-month partnerships with top 50 merchants
- **Behavioral moat:** Once users hold AED 50-100 in wallet, switching cost is high

- Own Marina + Downtown Dubai (density) before Careem notices
- By the time they launch (12-18 months), we'll have 50K+ users and behavioral data they can't replicate

**Risk 4: Regulatory Changes (UAE Central Bank Tightens Wallet Rules)**

**Likelihood:** Low (UAE is fintech-friendly, but possible)

**Mitigation:**
- Partner with licensed payment provider (Network International / Checkout.com) from Day 1
- Apply for UAE Central Bank SVF license in Month 6 (AED 50K fee, 6-9 months approval)
- Keep funds in segregated accounts (not commingled with operating cash)
- Legal counsel on retainer for compliance reviews

**Contingency Plan:**
- If SVF license denied ‚Üí switch to bank-issued wallet (Emirates NBD, FAB)
- If wallet regulations ban stored value ‚Üí pivot to instant bank cashback (direct deposit, no wallet)

---

**Risk 5: Merchant Churn >20% (Supply Side Failure)**

**Likelihood:** Medium (20-30% if ROI isn't clear)

**Mitigation:**
- Merchant dashboard with real-time GMV tracking (show ROI daily)
- Target merchants with high repeat frequency (cafes, salons) not one-time (furniture stores)
- Commission only charged on completed transactions (no upfront risk for merchants)
- Monthly merchant NPS surveys (catch churn early)

**Go/No-Go Criteria:**
- If merchant churn >15% after 90 days ‚Üí improve dashboard, add ROI reporting
- If merchant churn <10% ‚Üí expand merchant base
