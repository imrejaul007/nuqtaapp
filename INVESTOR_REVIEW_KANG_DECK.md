## DEEP DIVE CONCERNS

### 1. Merchant Commission (15%) is Too High ‚ö†Ô∏è
**Issue:**
- Payment processors charge 2-3%
- Aggregators charge 25-30% but provide delivery/logistics
- You charge 15% for cashback only

**Question:**
- Why would a merchant pay 15% when they can run their own loyalty (1-2% cost)?
- What's the ROI proof for merchants?

**Recommendation:**
- Need merchant case study showing ROI
- Or reduce commission to 5-8% (still profitable with 2.5-4% to you)

---

### 2. Single Founder Risk üö®
**Issue:**
- Solo founder, no CTO, "hiring with funding"
- 80% of successful fintech have 2+ co-founders

**Question:**
- Why go solo? Did co-founder leave?
- Who will build the tech while you're doing BD?

**Recommendation:**
- Bring on technical co-founder before raising
- Or show strong technical advisor/fractional CTO

---

### 3. Competitive Moat is Weak
**Issue:**
- Banks can copy in 3-6 months
- Tabby/Spotii already have distribution
- No proprietary tech mentioned

**Question:**
- What prevents Careem from launching "Careem Cash" with same model?

**Recommendation:**
- Emphasize speed to market
- Build exclusivity with merchants (1-year contracts?)
- Focus on "first mover in merchant-led cashback"

---

### 4. Traction is Too Thin
**Issue:**
- 200 waitlist is nothing
- 30 MOUs are non-binding
- No MVP, no pilot data

**Question:**
- Why not build MVP first, then raise?

**Recommendation:**
- Launch lean MVP in 1 month
- Get 50 real transactions
- Show proof of 30% share rate
- Then raise at higher valuation

### 5. Unit Economics Assume Best Case
**Issue:**
- $27 CAC assumes 50% merchant-led (near-zero cost)

- What if only 20% comes from merchant-led?

**Recommendation:**
- Show CAC sensitivity analysis
