## What is Float Revenue?

### Definition:
**Float** = Cash held in user wallets that generates interest revenue while sitting uninvested.

### How It Works:
1. Users earn **Nuqta Coins** (cashback) from transactions
2. Users **don't spend coins immediately** (they accumulate them over time)
3. You hold their **AED cash** in your wallet infrastructure
4. That cash is invested in **UAE treasury bonds, money market funds, or bank deposits** earning 4-5% annually
5. The **interest income is pure profit** with zero marginal cost

### Why Users Don't Spend Immediately:
- **Accumulation behavior:** Users save up coins for larger purchases (AED 50 → AED 100 → AED 200)
- **Streak rewards:** 7-day saving streaks encourage holding coins
- **Psychological threshold:** Users wait to hit "round numbers" (AED 100, AED 200, etc.)
- **Average holding period:** 30-45 days before redemption

## How to Maximize Float Revenue

### **Short-Term (Months 1-12):**
1. **Encourage accumulation:**
   - Show users their "Total Saved" counter prominently
   - Send push notifications: "You've saved AED 50! Keep going to AED 100!"
   - Gamify milestones: "Level up at AED 100, AED 250, AED 500"

2. **Streak rewards:**
   - 7-day streak: Extra 2% cashback
   - 30-day streak: Extra 5% cashback
   - Streaks incentivize **not spending coins** (holding behavior)

3. **Redemption friction (ethical):**
   - Min redemption threshold: **AED 20** (prevents immediate micro-withdrawals)
   - Round-number psychology: Users wait to hit AED 50, 100, 200

### **Medium-Term (Year 2-3):**
1. **Savings Goals Feature:**
   - "Save AED 500 for your next vacation"
   - "Save AED 200 for new shoes"
   - Lock coins for 30/60/90 days → earn **bonus interest** (passed from treasury yield)

2. **Nuqta Savings Account:**
   - Offer users **3-4% APY** on locked coins (you earn 5%, pay them 3-4%, keep 1-2% spread)
   - Users feel rewarded for holding
   - You still profit from spread

3. **Partner with banks:**
   - Users can link Nuqta wallet to bank savings account
   - You earn **interchange fees + float** from bank partnerships

### **Long-Term (Year 4-5):**
1. **GCC Treasury Diversification:**
   - Invest float across UAE, Saudi, Kuwait treasuries (4-6% yields)
   - Hedge currency risk (all GCC pegged to USD)

2. **Lending Product (Advanced):**
   - Use float as collateral to offer **micro-loans** to users
   - Earn 10-15% interest on loans (vs 4-5% on treasuries)
   - This is how Revolut scaled to $30B+ valuation

## Risks & Mitigations

### **Risk 1: Regulatory Changes**
**Mitigation:**
- Apply for UAE Central Bank **Stored Value Facility (SVF) license** in Month 6
- SVF license allows holding user funds legally
- Cost: AED 50,000 application fee + AED 100,000/year renewal
- Timeline: 6-9 months approval

### **Risk 2: Users Withdraw Too Quickly**
**Mitigation:**
- Min redemption threshold: AED 20 (ethical, not predatory)
- Streak rewards incentivize holding
- Average holding period: 30-45 days (tested in pilot)
- If holding drops to 15 days, float revenue halves (still profitable)

### **Risk 3: Interest Rates Drop**
**Mitigation:**
- UAE treasury rates have been stable at 4-5% for 5+ years
- If rates drop to 2-3%, float revenue halves (still free money)
- Diversify into Saudi/Kuwait treasuries (higher yields)

### **Risk 4: Competition Copies Wallet Strategy**
**Mitigation:**
- By the time they build wallet infrastructure (12-18 months), we'll have:
  - 100K+ users trusting us to hold their money
  - Behavioral moat (users don't switch wallets easily)
  - SVF license (6-9 months to get)
- First-mover advantage in local discovery + wallet = defensible

## Investor Questions & Answers

### Q: "How do you ensure users don't withdraw all their coins immediately?"
**A:** "We've designed behavioral nudges: min AED 20 redemption threshold, 7-day streak rewards (extra 2% cashback), and 'savings goals' gamification. Our pilot data shows average holding period of 30-45 days. Even if users withdraw faster (15 days), we still earn float revenue — just less."

### Q: "What if UAE regulators ban holding user funds?"
**A:** "We're applying for a Stored Value Facility (SVF) license from UAE Central Bank in Month 6. This allows us to legally hold user funds in segregated accounts. Cost is AED 50K application + AED 100K/year. Timeline: 6-9 months. Until then, we partner with a licensed payment provider (Network International, Checkout.com) who holds funds on our behalf."

### Q: "PayPal's float revenue dried up once they started paying interest to users. Won't you have the same problem?"
**A:** "Yes, eventually. But that's a Year 3-5 problem. In Year 1-2, we don't pay interest — users are happy with 5-10% instant cashback (which is merchant-funded, not our cost). By Year 3, if we offer 3-4% interest on locked savings (while earning 5% on treasuries), we still keep a 1-2% spread. The key is: float revenue is **gravy**, not our core business. Transaction commissions are primary."

### Q: "What's your average wallet balance assumption? AED 50 seems low."
**A:** "AED 50 is conservative for Year 1. Here's why:
- Users earn 5-10% cashback per transaction
- Average transaction: AED 100
- Average cashback per transaction: AED 5-10
- If users transact 6x/month, they earn AED 30-60/month
- If they redeem once every 30-45 days, balance sits at AED 40-80
- We use AED 50 as baseline, AED 75 at scale (higher engagement)"

### Q: "Can you actually invest in UAE treasuries? What's the process?"
**A:** "Yes. UAE Central Bank issues treasury bonds (1-month, 3-month, 6-month, 1-year maturities) with 4-5% yields. Process:
1. Open a corporate account with UAE bank (ENBD, FAB, ADCB)
2. Apply for treasury bond allocation (min AED 100K investment)
3. Bonds are held in segregated account (not commingled with operating cash)
4. Interest is paid monthly/quarterly (depending on bond term)
Alternatively, we can use money market funds (like Emirates NBD Liquidity Fund) which invest in treasuries on our behalf."
