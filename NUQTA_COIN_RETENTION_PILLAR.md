## 2Ô∏è‚É£ THE BEHAVIORAL PSYCHOLOGY STACK

### **Layer 1: Endowment Effect**

**Concept:** People value what they already own more than what they don't.

**How Nuqta uses it:**
- User has 47 Nuqta Coins in wallet
- Feels like **"their money"** (even though not yet redeemed)

**Result:** User keeps app to "not waste" accumulated coins

**Data to measure:**

### **Layer 2: Goal Gradient Effect**

## 8Ô∏è‚É£ INVESTOR OBJECTION: "What If Users Cash Out and Leave?"

### **Question:**
"Users will earn 50 coins, redeem AED 50, then delete the app. How do you prevent this?"

### **Answer:**

**Three mechanisms prevent cash-out churn:**

**1. Optimal Redemption Thresholds**
- Don't let users redeem everything at once
- Tiered redemptions: 10 / 30 / 60 coins (not "redeem all")
- **After redeeming 60 coins, user still has 8 coins left** (sunk cost keeps them active)

**2. Brand Coin Lock-In**
- User has 50 Nuqta Coins (universal) + 25 Brand Coins across 3 merchants
- If they redeem all Nuqta Coins, **they still have 25 Brand Coins locked** to specific merchants
- Must return to those merchants to use Brand Coins

**3. Post-Redemption Bonus**
- After redeeming AED 60, user gets: "Congrats! Here's 5 bonus coins to keep saving"
- Immediate restart of accumulation cycle
- **Prevents "zero balance churn"**

**Result:** Redemption is not an off-ramp, it's a milestone in an ongoing cycle.
